Cépage — bubble chart plotting grape varieties by body and acidity, with bubble size showing sweetness and, for reds, border thickness showing tannin

Body, acidity, sweetness — at a glance.

Position shows body (x) and acidity (y). Bubble size shows sweetness. Color groups grapes by fruit family. Hover any bubble for exact values.

White and red grape varieties plotted by body and acidity, sized by sweetness.
Bubble size = sweetness Border thickness = tannin

Positions are calibrated from WSET's standard descriptive bands (light to pronounced), not a single numeric source. Relative position between grapes is more reliable than exact coordinates. A few grapes appear twice where style materially changes the profile — oaked vs. unoaked Chardonnay, dry vs. sweet Chenin Blanc and Sémillon, cool vs. warm-climate Syrah.
